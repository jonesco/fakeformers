# G1 Finish

This document controls rendering, materials, and presentation only. Preserve the transformation architecture established by the other selected sources.

## Era and presentation

Create a mid-1980s Generation 1-style transforming action figure catalog photograph on an 8.5 × 11 inch portrait canvas.

Use:

- A seamless warm-white studio background
- Soft diffused overhead lighting
- Minimal specular highlights
- Soft, shallow contact shadows
- Slightly soft product focus and a faint suggestion of period film grain
- A straightforward, evenly lit catalog composition rather than dramatic or cinematic staging

The result should read as a real 1984–1986 mass-market transforming toy, not a modern collectible figure or photoreal CGI asset.

## Construction and materials

Use especially chunky, blocky proportions with simplified geometry. Favor a small number of large, smooth molded panels; use minimal panel lines and no decorative mechanical complexity.

All corners and edges are rounded or gently chamfered. Build the toy from matte-to-satin ABS plastic with occasional structurally plausible die-cast metal. Show simple hinge joints, visible Phillips screw holes at major joints, limited articulation, faint mold seams, and subtle injection-molding marks.

Keep the supplied subject's palette recognizable, but render it slightly muted and desaturated. Light plastic may show faint age-yellowing, and small painted details may have a slight, period-appropriate unevenness. When consistent with the user's requested colors and the actual alternate-mode parts, restrained red accent stripes may appear as flat vinyl stickers with a subtle registration offset. Never add red merely as an unexplained robot-only color.

Do not use painted gradients. Do not add rust, dirt, scratches, chipped paint, battle damage, or heavy wear; the toy is old but well preserved.

## Presentation

Use clear three-quarter catalog views. Robot mode has a broad, stable defensive stance consistent with limited 1980s articulation whenever the subject specification supports it.

Show only the two required modes. Do not add logos, packaging, catalog text, labels, arrows, borders, insets, unrequested loose accessories, transformation sequences, or additional renderings.

Avoid hyperdetailed or photoreal CGI, modern Masterpiece-scale engineering, sharp edges, intricate panel lines, dense greebling, glossy metallic surfaces, high-gloss lacquer, ray-traced reflections, complex articulation, and contemporary mecha styling.

If a surface could be one large soft-cornered molded piece, make it one large soft-cornered molded piece.
