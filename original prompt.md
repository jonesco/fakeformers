∙ Fighter jet: fighter pilot helmet silhouette with visor and intake-shaped cheek guards
∙ Race car: aerodynamic crest, racing helmet proportions, visor-style optic band
∙ Submarine: heavy rounded crown, porthole-style optics, pressure-seal faceplate
∙ Construction vehicle: hardhat-derived crown and industrial visor

The face, visor, helmet shape, crests, and antennae must reinforce the alternate form’s identity through suggestion and design language.

Use:
∙ Angular shapes
∙ Surface textures
∙ Color accents
∙ Silhouette echoes
∙ Mechanical references

Do not replicate the animal or vehicle’s actual face or form.

A robot that transforms into a shark should make you think of a shark. It should not look like a shark wearing a robot costume.

═══ HANDS AND GRIP DESIGN ═══

Robot hands are solid one-piece molded plastic forms.

Do not use:
∙ Articulated fingers
∙ Individual finger joints
∙ Knuckle detail
∙ Posed finger positions
∙ Realistic human hands

Hands should read as a single sculpted unit, like a real 1980s die-cast toy.

If the robot holds a weapon:
∙ The hand has a molded grip hole or clip
∙ The weapon pegs into the hand
∙ The weapon sits in the hand
∙ The weapon is not gripped by wrapped fingers

Hands must be accounted for in the manifest or stored inside a named alt-mode cavity.

═══ WEAPON OR HELD OBJECT ═══

The robot may hold a weapon that fits the world, profession, combat role, or theme of its alternate form.

Priority order:

1. A real, recognizable weapon thematically linked to the alt mode.

Examples:
∙ Tank: heavy cannon, autocannon, or large-caliber rifle
∙ Fighter jet: missile launcher or rail gun
∙ Fire truck: fireman’s axe
∙ Police car: service pistol or riot baton
∙ Shark or predator animal: trident, harpoon, or clawed gauntlet
∙ Construction vehicle: sledgehammer or pneumatic jackhammer
∙ Samurai-themed bot: katana
∙ Submarine: torpedo launcher or naval-style sidearm
∙ Dragon: sword, lance, flame-styled blaster, or heavy axe
∙ Insect: stinger blade, energy pistol, or clawed gauntlet

2. If no real weapon clearly fits, use a sci-fi blaster, rifle, or energy weapon styled to match the alt mode’s color and design language.

Do not use:
∙ Random vehicle parts as weapons
∙ Mufflers
∙ Exhaust pipes
∙ Hubcaps
∙ Tape dispensers
∙ Packages
∙ Pit tools
∙ Wrenches
∙ Improvised joke objects
∙ Generic spears, tridents, or polearms unless the alt mode is genuinely aquatic, hunting-related, mythological, or medieval-themed

The weapon should look like it was designed for combat first, with thematic styling second.

The weapon may appear in robot mode only, but it must be accounted for in the manifest as an accessory.

The weapon is not part of the transforming body unless explicitly stated.

If the weapon is formed from the alternate mode, then its source part must be named in the manifest and visible in both modes.

If the weapon is a separate accessory, it should be shown only in robot mode and should not be counted as part of the transformation body.

Do not mount, strap, clip, or attach the separate accessory weapon to the alt form unless the subject specifically requires it.

═══ ROBOT POSE ═══

The robot pose may be dynamic, but transformation readability is more important than action.

Do not choose a pose that hides major parts, obscures limb mapping, or makes color and part correspondence hard to verify.

Prefer a clear three-quarter action pose where these are visible:
∙ Chest
∙ Arms
∙ Legs
∙ Head
∙ Major backpack parts
∙ Wheels
∙ Wings
∙ Tail
∙ Shell
∙ Signature panels
∙ Creature limbs
∙ Vehicle panels
∙ Object casing panels

Vary the pose meaningfully across generations. Pick one that suits the character’s role and alt mode.

Pose options include:
∙ Mid-stride running or charging forward, body angled
∙ Crouched or kneeling, weapon braced or aiming
∙ Leaping or airborne, limbs trailing
∙ Mid-transformation twist, torso rotated against hips
∙ Two-handed weapon grip, braced for recoil
∙ Weapon held low or across the body
∙ Off-hand engaged, such as pointing, blocking, gripping a second tool, or shielding
∙ Turning over the shoulder, looking back
∙ Three-quarter rear view with head turned toward camera
∙ Heroic landing pose, one knee down, fist or weapon planted

Avoid:
∙ Symmetrical wide-leg standing stances
∙ Weapon held straight out to one side with the off-hand hanging limp
∙ Both arms locked at the same height
∙ Front-facing at-attention poses
∙ Showroom poses
∙ Poses that hide mismatched parts
∙ Poses that obscure limb origins
∙ Poses that make transformation verification impossible

The pose should suggest motion, weight, and intent, but not at the cost of engineering clarity.

═══ BODY ORIENTATION AND FRAMING ═══

Vary the composition meaningfully.

Pick one:
∙ Body angled to viewer’s left, head turned right
∙ Body angled to viewer’s right, head turned left
∙ Three-quarter rear view, head turned back toward camera
∙ Low-angle shot looking up at the robot, body angled
∙ Slight overhead angle looking down, robot crouched or moving
∙ Foreshortened pose with one limb thrust toward the viewer

Avoid:
∙ Body squared head-on to the camera in a planted stance
∙ Symmetrical front-facing hero shot compositions
∙ Default body-angled-right with weapon extended outward
∙ Angles that hide major transformation parts

The camera should feel like it caught the robot mid-motion from an interesting angle, while still keeping the transformation readable.

═══ PRE-RENDER ENGINEERING AUDIT ═══

Before rendering, silently audit the design as if inspecting a real prototype.

Ask:

1. Could a toy engineer build this transformation with the visible parts shown?
2. Can each robot limb be traced to a specific alt-mode region?
3. Can each alt-mode limb, wheel, wing, tail, head, shell, canopy, hood, casing, or body panel be traced to a specific robot region?
4. Do left and right parts stay on the correct side?
5. Do paired limbs keep their colors and markings?
6. Are there any generic robot parts that do not come from the alternate form?
7. Are there any alternate-form parts that vanish in robot mode?
8. Does the robot look like the alternate form rearranged, rather than a separate robot with decorative references?
9. Is at least 70% of each major manifest part visible in both modes?
10. Does every major part retain at least three visual identifiers across both modes?

If any answer fails, revise the design before rendering.

Do not render a best effort. Fix the transformation first.
