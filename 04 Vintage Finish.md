# Vintage Finish

This document controls rendering, background, lighting, and presentation only. Preserve the transformation architecture established by the other selected sources.

## Page and photography

Create an authentic 1984–1986 toy-catalog product photograph on an 8.5 × 11 inch portrait canvas.

Use:

- A seamless warm-white studio background
- Soft diffused overhead lighting
- Shallow contact shadows
- Slightly soft product focus
- A faint suggestion of period film grain
- Minimal specular highlights
- Straightforward catalog composition rather than cinematic drama

The result should look like a real mass-market toy photographed during the mid-1980s, not a modern collector figure or contemporary CGI recreation.

## Construction and materials

Use chunky, blocky proportions and simplified geometry.

Surfaces should read as matte-to-satin ABS plastic with occasional structurally plausible die-cast metal. Use large smooth panels, rounded or chamfered edges, simple pinned hinges, visible Phillips screw holes at major joints, and limited articulation typical of the period.

Small manufacturing imperfections are welcome:

- Faint mold seams
- Subtle injection marks
- Slight yellowing on light-colored plastic
- Minor paint registration error on small details
- Flat vinyl stickers with a subtle edge or registration offset

Keep colors slightly muted and desaturated. Markings are flat paint masks or stickers, never painted gradients.

Do not add rust, dirt, scratches, chipped paint, battle damage, or heavy wear. The toy may look old, but it should remain well preserved.

## Presentation

Use clear three-quarter catalog views. Robot mode should have a simple stable display pose consistent with limited 1980s articulation.

Only the two required modes are visible.

Do not add logos, packaging, catalog text, labels, arrows, borders, insets, unrequested loose accessories, transformation sequences, or additional renderings.

Avoid modern Masterpiece-style engineering, dense panel lines, intricate greebles, sharp knife edges, glossy metallic finishes, complex articulation, ray-traced reflections, and modern photoreal CGI materials.

If a surface could be one large smooth molded piece, make it one large smooth molded piece.
