# Negative Prompt

These are cross-cutting failure exclusions. They do not override an explicit requirement of the user's request, selected Finish, or selected subject-type document.

Avoid:

- Generic humanoid robot with subject parts attached afterward
- Two separately designed modes with only cosmetic similarities
- Magic transformation or unexplained mass shifting
- Floating, detached, duplicated, missing, or identity-changing parts
- Mismatched component counts
- Swapped left and right parts
- Mode-specific colors or markings
- Fake chest panels that imitate rather than reuse an alternate-mode surface
- Major parts concealed solely to hide inconsistency
- Bayformers-style fragmentation
- Gundam or modern anime-mecha aesthetics
- Dense greebling and tiny panel fragments
- Thin fragile appendages
- Excessive exposed mechanisms
- Articulated human hands
- Extra modes, characters, alternate concepts, or transformation stages
- Text, labels, captions, callouts, arrows, dimensions, title blocks, or specification boxes
- Exploded views, detail insets, material swatches, decorative borders, or background props
- Weathering, battle damage, grime, or dramatic cinematic effects unless explicitly requested

Avoid robot-head failures:

- Headless or faceless robot
- Missing neck or head mounting
- Face embedded in the chest
- Eyes placed only on the torso
- Reused stock robot head or a face that could be swapped unchanged onto an unrelated subject
- Default boxy crown, horizontal blue visor, flat silver faceplate, and paired vertical ear fins with no subject-specific justification
- Alternate-mode animal head, cockpit, hood, shell, mantle, snout, nose, or casing used as the entire robot head
- Robot head completely hidden by subject anatomy or shell structure

Avoid transformation-evidence failures:

- Subject-defining anatomy, equipment, controls, windows, eyes, markings, or functional parts omitted from robot mode
- New robot limbs with no visible alternate-mode source
- Alternate form rendered as an uninterrupted shell with no plausible storage volume
- Pose, shadow, cropping, or perspective used to conceal incompatible parts
- Decorative vehicle, animal, or object motifs substituted for the real transformed components

Do not interpret “technical line art” or an orthographic robot pose as prohibited when Schematic Finish is selected. Do not interpret permitted graph paper as a decorative border when Sketch Finish is selected.
