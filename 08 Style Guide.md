# Style Guide

Use the design DNA of early-1980s Japanese and American transforming toys.

Reference qualities associated with Takara Diaclone, first-generation Transformers, Bandai Machine Robo, and GoBots without copying a specific existing character, logo, insignia, or proprietary design.

Prioritize:

- A strong readable silhouette
- Chunky toy-like proportions
- A small number of broad molded parts
- Straightforward hinge and pivot logic
- ABS plastic construction
- Restrained die-cast structure
- Limited era-appropriate articulation
- Simple solid hands
- Clear transformation seams
- Subject-derived shapes rather than generic robot armor
- A unique, subject-specific robot head silhouette and face language rather than a repeated default helmet

The toy should feel clever, sturdy, manufacturable, and visually legible at catalog size.

Avoid modern collector-toy complexity, contemporary cinematic robot styling, anime-mecha anatomy, fragile layered parts, and decorative mechanisms with no transformation purpose.
