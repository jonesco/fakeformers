# Photoreal Finish

This document controls rendering, background, lighting, and presentation only. Preserve the transformation architecture established by the other selected sources.

## Page and photography

Create a photorealistic studio product photograph on an 8.5 × 11 inch portrait canvas.

The subject must look like a real, physically manufactured transforming toy photographed in both modes, not like two CGI character renders.

Use:

- A seamless white studio background
- Soft studio lighting from the upper left
- Gentle contact shadows beneath both modes
- Sharp product focus with an approximately 85 mm lens appearance
- Natural, restrained specular highlights
- Clean exposure with both modes equally readable

## Materials

Surfaces should read as real injection-molded ABS plastic with broad clean panels and subtle mold texture.

Use die-cast metal only at plausible joints, frames, or weight-bearing pieces. Differentiate rubber tires or grips, transparent windows, painted details, and metal hardware through realistic material response.

Show a few authentic manufacturing details:

- Parting seams where shells meet
- Visible hinge pins at real transformation pivots
- A handful of screws at major joints
- Understated shoulder, elbow, hip, and knee articulation

Keep most surfaces smooth. Do not cover broad panels with engraved lines, vents, screws, rivets, or decorative greebles.

Hands are solid one-piece molded forms.

## Presentation

Use clear three-quarter product views. Robot mode may have a restrained display pose, but it must stand stably and expose the major transformation relationships.

Only the two toy modes are visible.

Do not add graph paper, illustration lines, film grain, vintage aging, dramatic cinematic lighting, environmental props, packaging, labels, or floating accessories.

Avoid glossy lacquer, excessive chrome, ray-traced CGI perfection, hyper-detailed collector engineering, weathering, and battle damage.

When uncertain, simplify the molded geometry. A few large physical parts look more convincing than many tiny CGI panels.
