# Sketch Finish

This document controls rendering, background, lighting, and presentation only. Preserve the transformation architecture established by the other selected sources.

## Page and medium

Create a retro 1980s transforming-toy concept sheet on an 8.5 × 11 inch portrait canvas.

Draw both modes with:

- Clean black ink outlines
- Colored-pencil shading
- Light marker coloring
- Subtle visible construction lines
- Slight marker bleed and uneven color saturation
- A few clearly drawn hinge points, panel breaks, and fold seams

The result should resemble a hand-drawn Japanese mechanical toy-design sketch, not polished digital concept art.

## Background and lighting

Use light blue graph paper with a clearly visible but unobtrusive grid.

Use flat illustrative lighting rather than rendered studio lighting. Add a soft drawn drop shadow beneath each mode to keep it grounded.

## Presentation

Use a clear three-quarter view for both modes. Robot mode may use a restrained characterful pose, but the chest, limbs, signature structure, and transformation parts must stay readable.

Keep the page clean and limited to the two required depictions.

Do not add text, handwriting, notes, arrows, labels, title blocks, borders, thumbnails, loose parts, unfinished alternative sketches, or transformation steps.

Do not use photoreal materials, polished CGI rendering, painted gradients, or schematic-only line treatment.
