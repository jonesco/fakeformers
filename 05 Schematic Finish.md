# Schematic Finish

This document controls rendering, background, line treatment, page layout, pose, and viewpoint only. Preserve the transformation architecture established by the other selected sources.

Its pose and viewpoint rules explicitly override the defaults in Shared Design Rules.

## Page and background

Create an 8.5 × 11 inch portrait technical sheet.

Use one consistent neutral technical-paper background: off-white, very pale blue, or pale gray. Add a faint drafting grid that remains secondary to the drawings.

Do not imitate a decorative blueprint poster, CAD presentation board, title block, specification sheet, or framed engineering document.

## Line treatment

Render both modes entirely as precise black or dark-ink technical line art.

Use:

- Thin uniform lines for surface detail
- Slightly heavier outer contours and major structural edges
- Fine dashed lines for selected hidden edges
- Light centerlines or construction guides where useful
- Crisp seams, shell breaks, hinge pins, screw holes, pivots, and folds

Keep most surfaces broad and clean. Show only engineering details that clarify toy construction or transformation.

Do not use color fills, grayscale shading, solid shadow areas, marker, colored pencil, gradients, lighting, highlights, cast shadows, photoreal materials, or three-dimensional CAD rendering.

Represent established markings and color blocks only as unfilled outlined shapes.

## Robot mode override

Show robot mode in a strict front orthographic technical-reference pose:

- Perpendicular to the viewer
- Upright, centered, and symmetrical in stance
- Head level and facing forward
- Feet shoulder-width apart and flat
- Arms straight down beside the torso
- One-piece hands beside the hips
- No rotation, foreshortening, gesture, action pose, or hero stance

Transformation parts and subject-defining structures must remain visible and accountable despite the formal pose.

## Alternate mode

Show alternate mode below in a clear three-quarter technical perspective using the identical line treatment.

Do not add a ground plane or shadow.

## Removable weapon exception

If the established design includes a removable weapon, do not place it in the robot's hands. One small separate orthographic line drawing of that weapon may appear beside the robot.

This is the only permitted additional accessory depiction. Do not invent a weapon to fill empty space. Integrated weapons remain part of the two primary modes.

## Exclusions

Do not include labels, text, numbers, dimensions, measurement arrows, callouts, captions, transformation arrows, exploded views, detail insets, additional angles, front-and-rear comparisons, material swatches, title blocks, specification boxes, decorative borders, or presentation graphics.
